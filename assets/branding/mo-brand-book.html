09 / Brand Guidelines

Do & Don't

Quick reference for maintaining brand consistency across all touchpoints.

Do

  • Use iridescent gradients for hero moments and key visuals
  • Keep photography natural, dewy, and diverse
  • Use "screen fatigue" instead of "dry eye syndrome"
  • Pair Unbounded for headlines with DM Sans for body
  • Use the pink + mint as the primary color pair
  • Let the bottle serve as a lifestyle accessory in shots
  • Embrace bold color — chartreuse, lavender, coral are friends
  • Speak like a friend, not a pharmacist

Don't

  • Use clinical/medical imagery (lab coats, sterile environments)
  • Apply heavy filters that flatten the iridescent quality
  • Mix more than 3 brand gradients in one composition
  • Use serif fonts for headlines (serif = editorial accent only)
  • Pair pink with red — it reads as "error" not "glow"
  • Show perfect, heavily retouched beauty shots
  • Use complex medical terminology in consumer-facing copy
  • Put the logo on busy, cluttered backgrounds without a backdrop
