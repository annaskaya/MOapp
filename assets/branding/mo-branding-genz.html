06 / Brand Voice
A Friend, Not a Pharmacist
We speak like a creative friend who happens to know about eye care. Bold, honest, slightly cheeky. Never clinical.

Playful

Light humor, wordplay, winks. We don't take ourselves too seriously — but we take your eyes seriously.

"your eyes are working overtime. give them a raise."

Direct

Short, punchy lines. No fluff, no jargon. Say "screen fatigue" not "dry eye syndrome."

"tired eyes? drop in. done."

Affirming

Positive without being toxic. Self-care is normal. You don't need fixing — you need refreshing.

"you're already glowing. we just add the shine."

Bold

Unapologetic about being a beauty brand that does eye care. Not hiding behind science — leading with feeling.

"eye drops that look good on your desk and in your selfie."
07 / Brand Rules
Do & Don't

Do

  • Always lowercase for the wordmark
  • Use solid flat colors — no gradients
  • Mix handwritten Caveat with Unbounded for personality
  • Use real eye photography as the brand motif
  • Speak like a friend, keep copy short and punchy
  • Embrace color clashing — pink on teal, lavender on yellow
  • Show diverse eye colors and skin tones in collages
  • Let the wordmark be the hero — big, bold, lowercase
  • Add hand-drawn scribbles or underlines for emphasis
  • Treat every surface as content — if it's photographable, design it

Don't

  • Never use UPPERCASE for the wordmark
  • No gradients — flat colors only
  • No clinical/medical imagery (lab coats, sterile shots)
  • No "dry eye syndrome" — say "screen fatigue" or "tired eyes"
  • Don't pair pink with red (reads as error)
  • No heavily filtered or retouched photos
  • No serif fonts for headlines (serif = editorial accent only)
  • Don't put the logo on cluttered or busy backgrounds
  • No corporate speak — "leveraging synergies" is not our vibe
  • Never make the brand feel like a pharmacy product
