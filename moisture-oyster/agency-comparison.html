Proposal 01

Sirka — What They're Offering

Who they claim to be

Sirka positions itself as a DTC-brand launch and affiliate-program agency. They name-drop playbooks from Onnit, AG1, and Everyday Dose, and past work on new-market launches for Uber and communication platforms for Coca-Cola, Budweiser, and UNICEF. They pitch themselves as full-service partners, not just consultants.

Scope of services (what's inside the $70K)

The offer bundles seven service areas into one engagement:

  1. Research — market / category / competitor research
  2. Strategy — positioning and go-to-market
  3. Funnel creation — acquisition funnels
  4. Retention setup — email flows (Klaviyo-style)
  5. CRO — conversion rate optimization
  6. Website development — build the storefront
  7. Media buying — running paid ads

Note: the offer does not itemize pricing per service. Creative production volume, UGC sourcing, SMS, subscription logic, and analytics dashboards are not explicitly called out.

Pricing structure

Core engagement (one-time)$70,000Paid per a payment schedule referenced in their Notion
Media-buying commission5% of ad spendOn top of the $70K
Ad budgetNot includedYou fund media spend separately
Post-launch support2% of gross profitFor 12 months, starting month 7 after launch

Their pitch — why work with them

  • Supplements-niche fluency: FDA/EFSA compliance, claims guidelines, Meta/Google ad moderation, account bans.
  • Amazon → DTC migration: claim they can shift demand to DTC via offers, bundles, subscriptions, and email without killing marketplace sales.
  • Profit-over-traffic framing: LTV, repeat purchase, subscription focus.
  • Turn-key launches: positioning, packaging, site, funnels, ads, analytics — plus logistics, production timelines, stock, and cashflow.
  • Trust-led brand building: expertise, evidence, UGC, reviews — rather than aggressive miracle claims.
Reality check: The Sirka proposal is opinionated but vague on deliverables. There's no cost-per-creative, no monthly ad volume, no CAC/LTV benchmarks, no website deliverable scope (custom vs. template, pages included), and no analytics stack definition. That's why you need the checklist below — to force every agency (including Sirka, if you re-engage them) to quote the same line items.
The Playbook

Standardized Agency Scope Checklist

Send this to every agency. Require line-item pricing and numbers, not adjectives. If they can't answer in specifics, that's the answer.

01

Strategy & Positioning

Important
  • Do you define brand positioning, core messaging, and ICP (ideal customer profile)?
  • Do you design the offer structure — bundles, subscription tiers, pricing strategy, AOV levers?
  • Deliverables: positioning doc, messaging framework, offer architecture?
  • Price for this module alone?
02

Creative Production (UGC / Ads)

Most critical
  • Do you produce UGC-style video ads for TikTok and Meta?
  • Cost per video — raw footage vs. edited deliverable?
  • How many creatives per week/month are included? (Industry benchmark: 10–30 ads/week for a serious DTC launch.)
  • Do you source creators (casting, briefing, shipping product) or only edit footage we provide?
  • Usage rights, revisions, whitelisting included?
  • Static creative, photography, lifestyle shoots — separate line items?
03

Paid Ads / Media Buying

Critical
  • Which platforms: Meta, TikTok, YouTube, Google Search/PMax, Pinterest?
  • Pricing model — flat monthly fee, % of spend, or hybrid? (Sirka: 5% of spend.)
  • Minimum monthly ad spend required to engage?
  • Typical CAC benchmarks they've hit in supplements / beauty / eye-care?
  • Typical ROAS targets by month 1 / 3 / 6?
  • Who owns the ad accounts and data — us or them?
04

Website Build

Important
  • Platform — Shopify, custom, headless? (Note: our current stack is GitHub Pages + vanilla HTML/JS.)
  • Custom design or template-based? How many unique pages?
  • What's included — PDP, collection, cart, checkout, account, blog, policy pages?
  • Timeline from kickoff to live?
  • One-time build fee vs. ongoing maintenance?
05

Funnels & Conversion Rate Optimization

Important
  • Do you build dedicated landing pages and full funnels, or just one homepage?
  • How many LPs per month included? What about advertorials / quiz funnels?
  • A/B testing framework — tool (e.g., Intelligems, GA4 experiments), cadence, reporting?
  • Setup cost vs. ongoing monthly fee?
06

Retention — Email, SMS, Subscription

Critical (this is where margin lives)
  • Klaviyo (or equivalent) setup: welcome flow, abandoned cart, abandoned browse, post-purchase, winback, VIP?
  • SMS platform (Attentive / Postscript) — setup + monthly management?
  • Subscription logic — app (Recharge, Skio, Loop), upgrade/downgrade/pause flows, churn rescue?
  • Campaign calendar — how many emails/SMS per month included?
  • Setup one-time cost vs. monthly retainer?
07

Analytics & Reporting

Important
  • What do you track — CAC, LTV, payback period, contribution margin, cohort retention, blended vs. platform ROAS?
  • Do you build a live dashboard (Triple Whale, Northbeam, custom Looker, etc.)?
  • Reporting cadence — weekly, monthly, ad-hoc?
  • Attribution model and how you handle iOS 14 / signal loss?
08

Amazon → DTC Demand Shift

Relevant if applicable
  • Past case studies of brands you've transitioned off Amazon without killing revenue?
  • Specific tactics — insert cards, PPC cannibalization tests, bundle gating, subscription-only SKUs?
  • How do you avoid MAP / TOS issues?
09

Timeline & Milestones

Important
  • Time from signed contract to soft launch (site live, first ads running)?
  • Time to first meaningful data (enough spend to read signal)?
  • Key deliverable milestones by week — what do we review at week 2, 4, 8, 12?
10

Total Cost Summary

Required
  • One-time setup cost
  • Monthly retainer
  • % fees (media buy, revenue share, profit share)
  • Minimum required ad spend (per month)
  • Estimated minimum creative budget (per month)
  • Exit / termination terms — can we pause, scale down, or leave?
How to Read the Proposals

Red Flags & Green Flags

🚩 Red flags

  • Can't quote cost per creative or monthly creative volume
  • No CAC or LTV benchmarks from comparable brands
  • Bundles everything into one price (no accountability per module)
  • Weak or absent retention / subscription offering
  • Revenue/profit share without a cap or exit clause
  • Vague on attribution and reporting
  • No named team (who actually does the work?)
  • All strategy, no execution muscle

✅ Green flags

  • Obsessed with creative volume (10–30 ads/week) and iteration
  • Talks in LTV:CAC and payback period, not clicks or impressions
  • Clear A/B testing framework with tooling named
  • Has case studies in supplements, beauty, or eye-care specifically
  • Named operators on the account (media buyer, creative strategist, retention lead)
  • Transparent modular pricing — easy to scale up or down
  • Strong retention + subscription playbook
  • Willing to start with a paid discovery / diagnostic before a full engagement
Framing for the Team

Recommended Minimum Stack

A full-service $70K agency is one path. The leaner path — often better early — is to assemble three specialists and own the strategy yourself:

RoleWhat they doRough market range
Creative engine UGC sourcing, scripting, editing, weekly ad volume $5K–$15K/mo retainer
or $300–$1,500/video
Media buyer Meta + TikTok + Google, testing frameworks, reporting $3K–$8K/mo or 10–15% of spend
Shopify + retention setup Site build, Klaviyo flows, SMS, subscription app $8K–$25K one-time
$1K–$3K/mo retention mgmt

Ranges are market-typical ballpark figures for DTC supplements/beauty brands — not guarantees. Use as a calibration check when reading proposals.

Bottom line: everything else — deep research reports, positioning workshops, fancy analytics dashboards, post-launch % of profit — is optional until you have traction to optimize. The three line items above are what actually generate revenue in the first 90 days.
