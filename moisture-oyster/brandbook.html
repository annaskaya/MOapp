Our Point of View

Moisture is for people who live on screens and refuse to let it show.

Cultural Tension

Screens gave us everything — careers, connection, creativity — while quietly destroying our eyes. Nobody addresses this in ways that feel real, cool, or urgent.

Core Belief

Eyes deserve the same intention as skin, gut, and mind. The clinical industry treated them medically. We treat them beautifully.

The Zag

Competitors say "relief."  Moisture says "identity."

“We get it. You're not sick. You're just living on screens — and your eyes are paying for it. We built Moisture because we were tired of reaching for bottles that made us feel like something was wrong with us.”

What We Stand Against

The belief that eye care must feel medical, ugly, or embarrassing.

The Old Model

40+ years of pharmacy aisle eye care. White boxes. Clinical language. Forgettable brands treating dry eyes as a diagnosis — not a reality of modern life.

The Assumption

That people accept ugly, clinical products because functionality is enough. That aesthetics don't matter for eye drops.

The Gap

Skincare, supplements, and oral care evolved from clinical to design-led. Eye care remained stuck. Until now.

StoryBrand BrandScript

The 7-Part Narrative

1. Character

A 25–38 year-old screen-native professional woman. Ambitious, design-conscious, intentional about self-care. 8–12 screen hours daily. Experiencing dryness, fatigue, end-of-day burn.

2. Problem

Villain: The screen-life tradeoff.
External: Dry, tired, strained eyes by 3pm.
Internal: Why must eye care feel medical?
Philosophical: It's wrong that the screen generation has only hospital-looking options.

3. Guide

Empathy: "We understand. You're not sick — just screen-living."
Authority: Clean, preservative-free, LA-designed, ophthalmologist-informed. Featured in beauty & lifestyle press.

4. Plan

Step 1: Choose your drop (screen-life formula)
Step 2: Build your routine (morning / midday / evening)
Step 3: Feel the difference

5. Call to Action

Direct: "Get Moisture"
Transitional: "Take the Screen Strain Quiz" — 60-second quiz, email capture, personalized recommendation.

6. Failure

Daily dryness worsens. Tired-eye look becomes permanent. $200/month skincare routine ignored while the most expressive part of her face ages fastest.
